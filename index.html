Introduction
Problem Statement
Access to safe drinking water with adequate iodine levels is a pressing issue in the Himalayan region, leading to a high prevalence of iodine deficiency disorders among the local population. Our challenge is to develop innovative and sustainable solutions that can effectively restore and maintain iodine levels in Himalayan water sources, ensuring improved public health outcomes and well-being for the communities living in this region
Iodine deficiency (ID) is a significant global issue, leading to preventable mental retardation and other health problems. In Himachal Pradesh, ID can have serious consequences, including miscarriages, stillbirths, brain disorders, impaired psychomotor development, speech and hearing impairments in fetuses. This is because thyroid hormones, crucial for brain development, are affected when the pregnant mother is iodine deficient, compromising the mental and physical development of the fetus. Addressing iodine deficiency is essential in Himachal Pradesh to prevent these health issues.
Solution Overview
In our strategy to combat iodine deficiency in the Himalayan region, we leverage technology and collaboration. Specifically, we advocate for the implementation of advanced water treatment technologies. Our approach incorporates robust monitoring systems and sustainable infrastructure while also fostering community empowerment. By approaching this issue from multiple angles, our objective is to enhance health outcomes and empower communities to assert control over their water resources.
